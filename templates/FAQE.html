What is the difference between a FAQ and a FAQ(E)?

The aim of an FAQ is to answer users' frequently asked questions, while a FAQ(E) is quite the opposite. It looks like a FAQ in the question-answer format, but the questions are carefully chosen by the developers to emphasize the strengths of their product.

What does PubCron do?

PubCron automatically retrieves new PubMed abstracts matching your personal query, sorts them by relevance and sends them to you by mail. It is a bit like personalized medicine without the medicine part.

How does that differ from PubCrawler?

Well, not so much really. Mostly, it's the Linux penguin in PubCrawler versus the red book of PubCron. I don't have anything against penguins by the way, it's just that I thought that a book would be more appropriate. Still, if you're a penguin type of person, you should definitely check out PubCrawler.

What do I need to use PubCron?

You need a Google account. This is how your identity is recorded by PubCron. This also defines the email address where the results will be sent to.

How can I unsubscribe from PubCron?

Fill in an empty query on your PubCron's query page.

How can I send relevance feedback?

You have to do it from the PubCron mails. You can specify whether an abstract is relevant or not with the radio buttons after the text. Check "No answer" if you do not want to give relevance feedback for a particular abstract. Then press one of the "Send feedback" buttons at the beginning or the end of the mail. Sending the information from your Gmail to PubCron will trigger a warning. I am not trying to hack the hell out of your personal information, don't worry. At least not for now.

What do the numbers next to the abstract title mean?

This is the abstract relevance score. If you never sent any feedback, they should all be 0. Otherwise, they tell you how the given text matches your taste. If the score is negative, the abstract looks like stuff you don't like. If it is positive it looks more like stuff you like.

Do I have to send feedback for all articles?

You do not have to send feedback at all, but I recommend you send feedback for at least one relevant and one irrelevant abstract. Otherwise the abstracts will come in a random order.

Why do I receive at most 40 articles?

Hands-on experience shows that the top 40 hits usually contain the most interesting abstracts (unless you never gave relevance feedback). Also, without this limit, the PubCron mail can become quite large, which is not cool for your mailbox and your mail client.

Why do I get a warning message in the PubCron mail?

PubCron is a free application powered by Google App Engine. In order to stay within the limits of the quotas on traffic and computer power, PubCron has a limit on the articles it retrieves from PubMed (currently 200). If your query routinely returns more than this number of hits, some articles will be ignored (i.e. they will not be ranked and not be returned in the PubCron mail). To avoid this, make sure your query is focused and does not return too many hits.

I gave relevance feedback. Why do the articles still have a null score?

You probably gave feedback for relevant articles only. PubCron can compute a relevance score only if you give feedback for both relevant and irrelevant abstracts.
