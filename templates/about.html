About

Hi, I am Guillaume Filion, the developer of PubCron. The project started as a quick way to get around with HTML, Python, databases and information retrieval for an internship student. Sure enough, this wasn't a revolution, but we had a lot of fun, and even if we developed it for our own purposes we decided to make it public, for whoever would find it useful.

As I write those lines, I am finishing an update of the interface. Since Google App Engine updated the runtime environment to Python 2.7 several features were broken, and the page looked ugly anyway. I don't plan to maintain PubCron very actively in the future, but who knows...

I personally find it very useful. In about a week time the articles I received were very relevant to my own research and taste. On several occasions this drew my attention to articles I would have otherwise missed. Still, I have to admit that receiving so many abstracts every day, of stuff that I cannot read for lack of time adds to my unconscious stress level. I feel rather relieved when there is nothing interesting on PubMed.

Anyway, feel free to use PubCron the way you like. The code is free, in case you want to build something from there. With the current update, it is even more or less readable.

Finally, since you are still reading, I would like to advertise my blog, where I talk mostly about data analysis and genetics, and the website of my lab where you will find more about the research we do.
